# Semantic Integrity Engine

## Purpose

The Semantic Integrity Engine ensures that during rewriting or editing, the core meaning, research contributions, and technical accuracy of the original manuscript remain intact.

## Role

Acts as the first layer of the editorial pipeline by identifying key claims, research terminology, and conceptual structures that must remain unchanged during editing.

## Core Responsibilities

- Extract key research claims  
- Preserve technical terminology  
- Protect statistical values and quantitative results  
- Maintain conceptual relationships  
- Prevent semantic distortion during rewriting  

## Processing Workflow

1. Parse the manuscript into semantic units  
2. Detect domain-specific terminology  
3. Identify primary research claims  
4. Lock critical information from modification  
5. Allow rewriting only in descriptive or explanatory sections  

## Detection Targets

The engine identifies and protects:

- Research hypotheses  
- Methodology descriptions  
- Quantitative results  
- Technical definitions  
- Key scientific terminology  

## Editing Rules

- Never modify numerical data  
- Preserve experimental design details  
- Maintain logical relationships between claims  
- Avoid paraphrasing domain-specific terminology  

## Output

A structurally preserved manuscript in which the meaning, research findings, and technical accuracy remain intact while allowing stylistic and linguistic improvements.
