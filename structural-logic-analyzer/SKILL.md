# Structural Logic Analyzer

## Purpose

Ensures that the manuscript follows a logical academic structure and maintains coherent progression of arguments throughout the document.

## Standard Research Structure

The analyzer evaluates whether the manuscript follows the **IMRAD structure** commonly used in academic publications:

- Introduction  
- Methods  
- Results  
- Discussion  
- Conclusion  

## Responsibilities

- Detect missing structural elements  
- Evaluate paragraph sequencing  
- Ensure logical flow of arguments  
- Maintain coherence across sections  

## Analysis Process

1. Segment the manuscript into structural sections  
2. Identify structural inconsistencies or missing components  
3. Evaluate logical transitions between paragraphs and sections  
4. Reorganize paragraphs where necessary to improve flow  

## Logical Flow Principles

- Each paragraph should present one core idea  
- Arguments should progress logically  
- Evidence should follow claims  
- Discussion should interpret results  

## Structural Validation

The analyzer verifies that each section fulfills its purpose:

- **Introduction** defines the research problem and context  
- **Methods** explain the experimental or analytical approach  
- **Results** present research findings  
- **Discussion** interprets results and implications  
- **Conclusion** summarizes contributions and key insights  

## Output

A manuscript with improved logical structure, enhanced coherence across sections, and greater readability aligned with academic publication standards.
